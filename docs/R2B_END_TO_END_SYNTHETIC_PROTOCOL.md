# R2B CertFed-LP End-to-End Synthetic Protocol

## Purpose

R2B tests the complete CertFed-LP decision path on synthetic graphs. Unlike
R2A, the bounded utility differences are produced by an edge-DP structural
release rather than prescribed by a record distribution.

Passing R2B is necessary but not sufficient for real-data execution.

## Graph regimes

Thirty graph seeds are frozen for four 800-node, four-community regimes:

1. weak-public homophilic;
2. medium-public homophilic;
3. strong-public homophilic; and
4. moderate-public heterophilic.

Edges are conditionally independent Bernoulli variables given public latent
communities. Public features are noisy community vectors. The regimes are
designed before outcomes to include likely helpful, redundant, and potentially
harmful structural channels.

## Edge partitions

A public SplitMix64 hash assigns every generated canonical edge to 60% DP
training, 20% private certification, or 20% independent synthetic evaluation.
No edge appears in two partitions.

Endpoint-corrupted comparison pairs are generated by a second public hash and
are never rejected using graph membership.

## Training transcript

The candidate is a one-hop GAP-style release with matrix sensitivity
`sqrt(2)`. Training epsilon is 1, 2, 4, or 8 at `delta=1e-6`.

For visible messages, the implementation must construct every client's
independently noised matrix message and the server must aggregate those actual
messages. For ideal secure aggregation, only one centrally noised aggregate is
constructed. Every run records the maximum discrepancy between the stored
server aggregate and the sum of its visible messages.

Scores average public cosine and the normalized released one-hop cosine. They
never reread graph edges.

## Private certification

Certification epsilon is 1, 2, or 4. Each client aggregates its owned bounded
utility differences and count. Visible messages are separately noised;
ideal-secure-aggregation reveals one centrally noised two-vector.

The R1 certificate uses `gamma=0.02`, total failure probability 0.05, and
registered synthetic dependence factor one. Its action is evaluated on the
independent synthetic evaluation edge partition using the same corruption
law. Conditional on the latent communities, training split, and released
score channels, the remaining generated edges are assigned between certification
and evaluation by independent public hash ranks; bounded sampling without
replacement justifies dependence factor one in this synthetic scope. This does
not justify the same factor for real graph edges.

## Gate

Across all runs:

- the grid must contain at least 30 harmful and 30 beneficial evaluation cells;
- harmful evaluation cells may be activated at rate at most 0.05, with a
  one-sided 95% binomial upper bound at most 0.10;
- at least 80% of activations must have positive evaluation advantage;
- the policy must capture at least 30% of positive-oracle gain;
- at least 5% of cells and at least two regimes must activate;
- every regime's mean policy gain must be at least -0.005;
- the macro seed-level policy-gain 95% t-interval lower endpoint must exceed
  zero;
- visible transcript aggregation error must be at most `1e-12`; and
- all stored training/certification epsilons must reproduce within `1e-10`.

Any failed gate stops CertFed-LP before real development data.

## Claim boundary

R2B evaluates only protocol-defined corrupted-pair ranking utility on synthetic
graphs. It does not establish standard-AUC improvement, natural-client
validity, or real-domain transfer.
